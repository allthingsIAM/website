    The gap nobody talks about

    You know the Entra ID syntax. You can spin up conditional access policies. But when someone asks you to design a tenant-wide identity governance strategy, you're stitching together answers from six different Microsoft docs, three Stack Overflow threads, and whatever you remember from that one Identiverse talk.

    That's not a skill problem. It's a framework problem.

    →

    This course teaches you how to think about Entra ID governance, not just how to click through the portal. Every module is built around the decisions you'll actually face — what to federate, when to automate, how to structure access for an org that keeps changing.

    Curriculum

    8 modules. No filler.

    Each module builds on the last — from tenant fundamentals to advanced enterprise scenarios.

    Module 01
    Foundations
    Tenant configuration, hybrid identity setup, and the mental models that separate theory from practice.
    Module 02
    Hybrid Identity & AD FS
    Federation patterns and when — and why — to replace AD FS with Entra Conditional Access.
    Module 03
    Authentication & Passwordless
    MFA, FIDO2, passkeys, and building authentication strategies that actually stick in the real world.
    Module 04
    Conditional Access
    Beyond the basics — designing risk-aware access controls that protect without breaking workflows.
    Module 05
    Lifecycle Workflows
    Offboarding pipelines, JML automation, and attribute management at scale.
    Module 06
    Enterprise Governance
    Dynamic groups, RBAC, and structuring IAM for organizations — not just environments.
    Module 07
    Governance & Auditing
    Compliance frameworks, Microsoft Graph reporting, and dashboards that show what's actually happening.
    Module 08
    Advanced Scenarios
    Tenant-to-tenant migrations, federation in complex orgs, and high-assurance environments.
